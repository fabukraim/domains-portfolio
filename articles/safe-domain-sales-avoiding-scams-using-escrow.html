Safe Domain Sales: Avoiding Scams and Using Escrow

By Timothy S. Krause

The Dark Side of Domain Trading

Domain investing attracts legitimate businesses and sophisticated scammers alike. In 2026, domain fraud costs investors millions annually. At DomanID, we prioritize transaction security, having witnessed countless preventable losses. This guide equips you with essential knowledge to protect your assets and execute safe sales.

Common Domain Scams

Understand the threats you face:

Fake Escrow Services

Scammers create counterfeit escrow websites mimicking legitimate services. They collect buyer payments but never release funds to sellers. Always verify escrow service authenticity through official channels.

Overpayment Scams

Buyers send fake checks or wire confirmations for amounts exceeding the sale price, requesting refund of difference. The original payment bounces days later, leaving sellers out of money and domain.

Phishing Attempts

Emails pretending to be from registrars or marketplaces request login credentials. Never share passwords or 2FA codes. Legitimate companies never ask for credentials via email.

Unauthorized Transfers

Scammers gain access to your registrar account and transfer domains without consent. Strong passwords and 2FA prevent this.

Identity Theft

Fraudsters use stolen identities to purchase domains, then dispute charges after receiving assets. Sellers lose domains and face chargeback fees.

Escrow: Your Safety Net

Escrow services protect both buyers and sellers:

How Escrow Works

  1. Buyer and seller agree on terms
  2. Buyer deposits funds with escrow service
  3. Escrow verifies fund receipt
  4. Seller transfers domain to buyer
  5. Buyer confirms receipt
  6. Escrow releases funds to seller

This process eliminates trust requirements between parties.

Recommended Escrow Services

  • Escrow.com: Industry standard, ICANN-accredited
  • Dan.com: Integrated marketplace escrow
  • Sedo: European-based escrow services
  • PayPal Goods & Services: For smaller transactions (with caution)

At DomanID, we integrate Escrow.com for all high-value transactions.

Verifying Buyer Legitimacy

Before transferring domains:

Identity Verification

  • Request government-issued ID for high-value sales
  • Verify email addresses match company domains
  • Check LinkedIn profiles for consistency
  • Search for negative reviews or scam reports

Payment Verification

  • Wait for funds to clear completely (not just pending)
  • Confirm wire transfers directly with your bank
  • Avoid cryptocurrency unless using specialized escrow
  • Never accept checks for domain transactions

Communication Red Flags

  • Urgency pressure ("Must close today")
  • Unusual payment methods
  • Refusal to use escrow services
  • Poor grammar in professional contexts
  • Requests to communicate outside platform

Securing Your Registrar Account

Protect your domain portfolio:

Strong Authentication

  • Use unique, complex passwords (16+ characters)
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Use authenticator apps, not SMS (SIM swapping risk)
  • Change passwords quarterly

Account Monitoring

  • Review login history regularly
  • Set up login notifications
  • Monitor domain transfer logs
  • Keep contact information current

Domain Locking

  • Enable registrar lock on all domains
  • Require authorization codes for transfers
  • Disable auto-transfer settings
  • Use privacy protection to hide WHOIS data

Transaction Documentation

Document every sale:

  • Written agreement outlining terms
  • Escrow transaction ID and receipts
  • Email correspondence records
  • Transfer confirmation screenshots
  • Payment verification documents

Proper documentation protects against disputes and chargebacks.

International Transaction Considerations

Cross-border sales carry additional risks:

  • Currency conversion fees and fluctuations
  • Different legal jurisdictions
  • Banking regulations and restrictions
  • Time zone communication challenges

Use international escrow services familiar with multi-jurisdiction transactions.

Case Study: Escrow Prevention

A seller received $50,000 offer via email. Buyer requested direct wire transfer, offering to cover fees. Seller insisted on Escrow.com. Buyer disappeared. Later, seller learned similar domains were scammed via direct transfer. Escrow requirement saved $50,000 loss.

What to Do If Scammed

If fraud occurs:

  1. Document all evidence immediately
  2. Contact escrow service (if used)
  3. Report to registrar for domain recovery
  4. File police reports in your jurisdiction
  5. Notify ICANN for trademark issues
  6. Warn community through forums

Quick action increases recovery chances.

Building Trust Reputation

Legitimate sellers attract serious buyers:

  • Use verified marketplace profiles
  • Maintain transaction history
  • Provide clear terms and conditions
  • Respond professionally to inquiries
  • Complete transactions promptly

Reputation reduces friction and scam suspicion.

Insurance Options

Consider domain insurance for high-value portfolios:

  • Theft and fraud coverage
  • Legal defense for disputes
  • Business interruption protection
  • Valuation coverage for losses

Insurance adds layer of financial protection.

Conclusion: Security Is Non-Negotiable

Domain investing rewards extend only to those who protect their assets. Scammers target the unprepared. By using escrow services, verifying buyers, securing accounts, and documenting transactions, you eliminate most fraud risks. At DomanID, security is embedded in every transaction we facilitate. Never compromise on safety for convenience. The few extra steps required for secure transactions protect years of investment effort. Stay vigilant, use escrow always, and trade with confidence.
